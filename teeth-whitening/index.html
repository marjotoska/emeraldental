Teeth Whitening - How harmful is it for your teeth?


Proper oral hygiene is important to keep your teeth healthy and strong in the long-term. But even brushing and flossing regularly can go so far.
One area where many of us fall short in improving oral health is taking caution with the foods we eat. In fact, making healthy choices in food types is important for not only fighting stained and discolored teeth, but also improving the overall health and white, shiny appearance of your teeth.
Read on to learn what kinds of food causes your teeth to stain, and what can you do to remedy it.

How do your pearly whites get stained?

Though we’re familiar with the fact that foods like coffee cause staining, we may not be aware of the reasons why teeth are more susceptible to certain types of foods. Teeth are made from several substances, including enamel, which is the outermost layer of the tooth. Enamel is a hard tissue that covers the tooth and acts as a protective layer.

Enamel is porous, which means it can absorb substances that it is exposed to. Over time, the absorption of substances into the tooth through the enamel can cause discoloration, or what we know as stained teeth. The more embedded certain substances become in teeth, the more discolored the teeth will end up. This is how teeth end up taking on a yellow or brown-colored tint.

 
 

Teeth Whitening Options

So how do we combat this discoloration? The answer is simple, by a whitening procedure! There are a number of options when considering whitening your teeth, such as:

  • Professional Dental Cleaning
  • Over-the-counter whitening strips
  • Whitening kits from online retalier shops

Most health professionals advise a professional dental cleaning in your dentist office, as your dentist is a certified professional who has experience over the years with this kind of treatment.
You can of course try at-home remedies, but the concentration of Hydrogen Peroxide(the whitening agent) is lower than at your dentist's, and you can also mess up and hurt your gums!



Professional teeth whitening

Our practice at Emerald Dental Clinic provides teeth whitening treatments that lift external stains from the enamel, brightening your smile with a single treatment. The process starts with protecting the gums and lips so that only the teeth are exposed during the procedure. A bleaching gel is applied and lightened with blue light to speed up the whitening process. The results are immediately noticeable.



Maintaining your results

Your eating, drinking, and oral hygiene habits can impact how long your teeth whitening results last. After you’ve completed any whitening treatment, your teeth are still susceptible to staining from beverages like tea and coffee, and certain foods. Rinsing your mouth or brushing your teeth soon after eating or drinking can keep those kinds of discoloring agents from settling into the surface of your teeth — and decrease the chance for plaque to build up!
